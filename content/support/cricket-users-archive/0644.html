Ivan:

Thanks for the note! However, I still think I'm missing something.
 If I use mtargets to aggregate four interfaces as you show below,
aren't I just adding up the information that already being
collected for those interfaces? In other words, assume that the
four interfaces are collecting and graphing ifInUcastPkts and
ifInNUcastPkts. One line of each graph with show ifInUcastPkts
while the other line will plot ifInNUcastPkts. If I then use
mtargets to aggregate these, won't I just get a new graph that
still shows one line for ifInUcastPkts and another line for
ifInNUcastPkts? How do I make a graph that plots a single line
that the sum of these two OIDs?

In short, it seems that mtargets will aggregate multiple interfaces
into a single graph... I'm just not sure I see how it can aggregate
multiple OIDs into a single line on a graph...

Thanks!
